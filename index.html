The Plastic Planet

Plastic has become both essential and destructive.Its rising production
and poor waste management are leaving a lasting mark on
the planet.

The Plastic Production

Per Capita Plastic Waste

Plastic waste production per person shows stark global differences.
Wealthier nations often produce more plastic waste per capita, reflecting
higher consumption levels. Yet, poorer regions face the harshest impacts
of this global pollution problem.

Global Plastics Production over the Years

Global plastic production has grown from around 2 million tonnes
in 1950 to more than 500 million tonnes in 2020.This steady rise
reflects how deeply plastics have become part of modern life, while
also pointing to the environmental pressures they continue to create.

Where Does It All Go?

Disposal of Plastic Waste

Over the past decades, most plastic waste has ended up in landfills,
while only a tiny portion has been recycled. This stark difference
highlights how production has far outpaced sustainable waste
management, leaving recycling efforts struggling to make a
significant impact on the planet's plastic burden.

MISMANAGEMENT & OCEAN POLLUTION

Mismanaged Plastic Waste per Capita

Mismanaged plastic waste per capita varies widely across countries,
revealing significant disparities in waste management infrastructure
and practices.Some nations generate high amounts per person due to
consumption patterns, while others, despite lower production,struggle
to manage waste effectively, contributing disproportionately to
environmental pollution.

Accumulated Ocean Macroplastics

Since 1950, the accumulation of ocean macroplastics has increased
significantly, starting at approximately 400 million tonnes. Over the
following decades, plastic waste entered the oceans at an accelerating
rate. By 2024, the total accumulated macroplastics reached around
1,403,900 million tonnes,illustrating a continuous and rapid upward trend.
This trend highlights the growing impact of human-produced plastic
on marine environments.

IMPACTS

Greenhouse Gas Emissions from Plastics

Plastics are a major source of greenhouse gas emissions, with carbon
dioxide making up the largest share. Every stage, from production to
disposal, releases far more carbon dioxide than other gases. This
underscores the urgent need for better waste management and
sustainable alternatives.

Rivers that Contribute the Most to Ocean Plastic Pollution

Rivers play a major role in transporting plastic waste to the oceans,
with the Pasig River in the Philippines contributing the highest at 6.4%
of global riverine plastic emissions.Most of this plastic comes
from mismanaged local waste, highlighting the need for improved
waste management and river cleanup.

Hope in Action

Share of Plastic Waste that is Recycled

Global recycling rates for plastic waste are gradually increasing,
indicating a positive shift in waste management practices. Despite
the overall proportion of recycled plastic remaining low, the upward
trend reflects growing implementation of recycling infrastructure,
policy interventions, and public awareness, suggesting potential for
measurable reductions in plastic-related environmental impacts over
time.
